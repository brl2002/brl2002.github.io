    Community Involvement


    As a member of IGDA (International Game Developers Association), I am very proactive in the game development community. I have volunteered for many game industry events so far, whether it is local or international. Internationally, I have volunteered at IGDA Summit 2013 and IGDA at GDC 2014. I have also volunteered for local events that happened in Toronto, including, Gamercamp 2013 and 2014, TOJam 2014, and Level Up 2014. I love being surrounded by game developers that have same interest as me. It allows me to know different people in the industry and as well as to learn from them. For this reason, I will continue being active volunteer in the game industry as a way of giving back to the community.
    Back in March, 2014, I formed an IGDA Academic Chapter for George Brown College with some of my colleagues. Purpose of forming the organization was to create a better learning environment for the students studying in game development programs and allow these students to network with one another. We hope to grow bigger and eventually be a representative of game development scene at George Brown College.


    IGDA awards scholarships to students that are most promising in game development related studies. Scholarships allow selected students to be able to attend major game industry events for free, and these include, GDC, GDC Europe, E3, TGS, and CEDEC. I have been selected as an IGDA Scholar E3 2014 back in June. While I was at E3, I was able to learn a great deal about console game development that I would have not been able to learn by sitting on my desk. It just was an amazing experience overall, and I met a lot of amazing people in the industry.
